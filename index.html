In my old neighborhood, I lived near a power plant, one that constantly emitted thick plumes of smoke. As I grew into the tech landscape, that memory became a constant reminder of the environmental cost of powering our digital world. The image stayed with me and has fueled my commitment to protecting the environment. My first push towards change was building a Recycle Bot at the University of California, Riverside (UCR), working to encourage sustainable practices. During my first research experience in networking power consumption with Professor Zhaowei Tan, I solidified the realization that this was my calling.

My research under Professor Zhaowei Tan involves investigating power consumption during data transfer in 5G networks using a Raspberry Pi. Due to the lack of native 5G connectivity in the Pi, I played a crucial role in designing a custom, portable testbed. Using my experiences in networking and robotics, I created a push-button-activated portable testbed to measure power consumption across different base network configurations. We conducted our measurements by going around campus and fixating on singular base networks; we then cross-referenced the different base networks to see which configurations would cause the most and least power consumption. This project revealed to me that speed and sustainability can coexist, despite the challenges still facing 5G technology

Throughout my journey at UCR, developing hands-on research projects involving networking and robotics has provided me with a robust technical foundation and the motivation to explore networking efficiency. Earning my B.S. in Computer Science has been transformative, yet I seek a deeper understanding of networking and robotics. My recent research endeavors have invigorated my passion.
